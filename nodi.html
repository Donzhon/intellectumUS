Nodi

Make STEM Visible and Interactive

Nodi is a hands-on STEM ecosystem that helps students explore physics, motion, coding, robotics, and engineering through real, modular hardware — turning abstract concepts into things students can build, see, and improve.

Request a Demo Explore the Ecosystem

Learn Engineering by Seeing It Work

Nodi turns complex engineering principles into visible, hands-on experiences. Students build, test, and improve real systems, coding, and motion — so engineering becomes intuitive instead of abstract.

Hardware in action

From physical structures to engineering thinking

What Students Learn

  • Physics in motion: forces, energy, and movement
  • Linear motion and actuators
  • Coordination and mechanical logic
  • Coding and algorithms
  • Robotics and navigation
  • Problem-solving and debugging
  • Design and systems thinking

Four connected learning systems

The Nodi Ecosystem

Mechanics & Structures

A visual, modular motion system for exploring ratios, torque, speed, and direction.

Motion & Actuators

Linear and rotational movement to demonstrate engineering principles in motion.

Coding & Control

A modular system for connecting hardware and controlling it through code.

Robotics & Systems

Combine motion, sensing, and code into autonomous robotic systems.

How Students Learn With Nodi

Observe. Connect. Code. Test. Improve.

  1. Explore

    See how a physical system behaves and ask why it works.

  2. Build

    Construct working models using real engineering principles.

  3. Code

    Program motion, logic, and behavior into the build.

  4. Improve

    Test, debug, and iterate in a design, program, redesign, re-engineering loop.
